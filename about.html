About Me

Overview

Interdisciplinary researcher/developer with experience in quantitative and qualitative research for interactive 3D computer graphics, human spatial ability, data visualization, virtual and augmented reality, serious games, 3D modeling and printing, documentary-style video production and direction, statistical analysis, and K-12 STEM outreach for teacher professional development. Experience connecting people at local, municipal, state, and federal levels to ensure project success. Extensive background in life sciences including plant physiology and molecular biology with associated laboratory techniques. Career interests include human-computer interactions and interfaces, applications of human spatial ability research to gaming and immersive educational experiences, data science and data-driven decision making, advanced data perception methodologies, technology integration for K-12 education, life science outreach, and public informal STEM education.

Recent Experience

Most recently, I worked at the Learning Systems Institute at Florida State University for 5+ years in research and development related to K-12 STEM teacher professional development. In this really interesting position, I studied and implemented novel methods for curriculum development and teacher professional development. Along the way, I developed educational standards maps, over 350 short-format documentaries, many 3D-printable models and associated lesson plans, over 40 interactive student tutorials, and many statistics training modules for science teachers (see code tab).

I partnered with an 8th-grade teacher and we collaborated to bring data visualization lessons to middle school students. Along with two other PCs, I mentored a group of undergraduate students over six weeks as they got first-hand research experience with NASA experts.

Teaching Philosophy

My approach to teaching involves knowledge, tools, connections, and experiences. I focus on building knowledge from existing understanding that each student brings with them into a learning experience, engaging students to utilize tools that can facilitate a more rapid internalization of ideas and methods, striving to create connections between the real world and the abstract concepts presented to students so that classroom discussions have tangible meaning to each learner, and engaging students in memorable experiences of discovery.

Our understanding of life, and how it functions, changes as new knowledge flows in from research. My philosophy involves attention to communication of current understanding and practices, but also instilling in students an ability and openness to receive new knowledge for incorporation into their existing understanding. As scientists, we must be receptive to changes in our fields, but must also maintain healthy skepticism. As part of learning about science, students must adopt practices which can carry them forward as lifelong learners and understand that knowledge is plastic and malleable.

A large focus of my instructional methodology is to provide students with the ability to engage in scientific discovery through the appropriate use of tools from diverse STEM fields and from technology, including both hardware and software. In this way, students are not only exposed to hands-on learning, but they also find that their knowledge of tools provides them with real-world skills that are marketable in their own right. I strive to stay current with regard to technology that may have broader impacts in classroom settings, even when the original intended application is for non-educational use. My recent work involves visual media, 3D printing, and statistical analysis coding to help convey abstract concepts, but also to train learners to create their own knowledge.

I think one of the best ways to incorporate knowledge is to relate new information to existing information, either by expansion, modification, or replacement of current understanding. For this reason, I try to understand what students know and use their own abilities to create common ground for connections to the new information they are learning. Typically, this happens through multiple examples until the connections occur, but sometimes I find it useful to have students reiterate their understanding in a different framework to explain the new information back to me in terms that make sense from the students point of view. Flipping the role of teacher and student allows students to develop the ownership of a particular concept and I think this drives a stronger internal connection that leads to learning and not just memorization.

While books and other published media offer certain advantages, research and communication experiences offer their own unique opportunity for unstructured problem and solution development. I believe that the practical application of academic knowledge offers students a chance to develop an internal framework for problem solving, while utilizing the knowledge and skills they learned about in the classroom. As part of completing scientific exercises, I expect students, and teachers, to employ their abilities and connect the dots from research question development to publication in some form. A strong capstone method I have used in teacher professional development involves creating teams of participants, which then jointly devise, complete, and present a research study while utilizing content knowledge, research methods, and data collection and analysis tools. During the communication experience, groups share how and why they studied their topic, but also learn about the shortcomings of their own work and how they might improve upon it at a later time. I find these experiences to be highly effective in not only creating an integrated learning environment for STEM content, but also in creating understanding about a more complete scientific process that bridges existing and new knowledge.

I believe the best result of teaching is a student prepared to learn beyond the current setting. As we strive to develop students into prepared lifelong learners, I feel we should use experiences and appropriate tools to create an environment where they can learn not only the established content specific to each discipline, but also a method for continuing their learning journey.
